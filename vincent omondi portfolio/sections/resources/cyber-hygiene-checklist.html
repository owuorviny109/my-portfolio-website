Cyber Hygiene Checklist

Maintaining good cyber hygiene is crucial for protecting your digital assets from common threats. Think of it as the digital equivalent of personal hygiene â€“ regular practices that keep you safe and secure online.

Key Checklist Items:

  • Strong, Unique Passwords: Use complex passwords for all accounts and avoid reusing them. Consider a password manager.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enable MFA wherever possible, especially for critical accounts like email, banking, and social media.
  • Software Updates: Keep your operating system, web browsers, and all applications updated to patch known vulnerabilities.
  • Secure Wi-Fi Networks: Use a strong password for your home Wi-Fi and ensure it's using WPA2 or WPA3 encryption. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
  • Phishing Awareness: Be cautious of unsolicited emails, messages, or calls asking for personal information. Verify the source before clicking links or downloading attachments.
  • Regular Backups: Back up your important data regularly to an external drive or a secure cloud service.
  • Firewall Usage: Ensure your computer's firewall is enabled.
  • Secure Browsing Habits: Look for "HTTPS" in website URLs, avoid suspicious websites, and be careful about what you download.
  • Limit Information Sharing: Be mindful of the personal information you share online and review privacy settings on social media.
  • Device Security: Secure your mobile devices with PINs, passwords, or biometric authentication. Install reputable security software.
