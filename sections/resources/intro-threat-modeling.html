Introduction to Threat Modeling

Threat modeling is a proactive approach to security where potential threats are identified, enumerated, and prioritized – all from a hypothetical attacker’s point of view. It helps in understanding how an attacker might be able to compromise a system and what defenses are needed.

Why is Threat Modeling Important?

By systematically analyzing your applications, systems, or processes, threat modeling allows you to:

  • Identify vulnerabilities early in the development lifecycle.
  • Prioritize security efforts and resource allocation.
  • Make informed decisions about security controls.
  • Reduce the overall attack surface and potential impact of breaches.
  • Foster a security-aware culture within development teams.

Common Threat Modeling Methodologies:

  • STRIDE: Focuses on Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, and Elevation of Privilege.
  • PASTA: Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis. A risk-centric methodology.
  • VAST: Visual, Agile, and Simple Threat modeling.
  • OCTAVE: Operationally Critical Threat, Asset, and Vulnerability Evaluation.

The choice of methodology often depends on the context, complexity of the system, and team familiarity.

Basic Steps in Threat Modeling:

  1. Define Scope & Assets: What are you protecting? What are the critical assets?
  2. Decompose the Application: Understand the components, data flows, and trust boundaries. Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs) are often used.
  3. Identify Threats: Using a methodology (like STRIDE per interaction point) or brainstorming, list potential threats.
  4. Document & Analyze Threats: For each threat, assess its likelihood and potential impact.
  5. Determine Mitigations: Identify and prioritize controls or changes to reduce or eliminate the threats.
